# What The Banks Say About AI

## Bottom line
- AI-related evidence was found for all 50 banks in the corpus.
- The corpus is dominated by transcripts (57.2%) and SEC filings (42.8%).
- The strongest recurring themes are measurable outcomes, investment spend, operations efficiency, customer facing ai, use cases, vendors partnerships.
- In plain terms, the banks mostly talk about AI as a productivity and operating-efficiency tool first, then as a customer-service and advisory tool, with governance and regulatory risk discussed mainly in SEC risk disclosures.

## Cross-bank pattern
- Most banks are not talking about AI as a single flagship product. They are talking about many smaller use cases across service, operations, risk, compliance, engineering, and advisor workflows.
- The more detailed and concrete AI discussions appear in earnings transcripts; the more cautious and risk-heavy language appears in 10-K and 10-Q filings.
- The most AI-dense banks in this corpus by mention count are MS, SF, AXP, COF, NTRS, SOFI, CFG, BAC, PNC, C.
- The thinnest AI evidence in this corpus is for UMBF, BKU, PNFP, EWBC, PB, which means conclusions for those names should be treated more cautiously.
